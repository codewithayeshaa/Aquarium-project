Marine Animals

Sea Turtle

Gentle marine reptiles known for their long migrations and ancient lineage. They can live for decades and play a vital role in marine ecosystems by maintaining healthy seagrass beds and coral reefs.

Great White Shark

A powerful predator known for its strength and important ecological role. These sharks help regulate species populations and are among the ocean's top predators, often traveling vast distances.

Dolphin

Highly intelligent and social marine mammals known for their playful behavior. Dolphins communicate with clicks and whistles and live in groups called pods, displaying strong social bonds.

Octopus

A master of camouflage and intelligence, with eight arms and a curious mind. Octopuses are escape artists with problem-solving abilities and can change both color and texture to hide from predators.

Jellyfish

Graceful yet dangerous creatures that drift with ocean currents. Despite having no brain or heart, jellyfish use their stinging cells to catch prey and have existed for millions of years.

Seahorse

Tiny and delicate, seahorses are known for their upright swimming and male pregnancies. They anchor themselves to seagrass with their tails and feed on tiny plankton and crustaceans.
